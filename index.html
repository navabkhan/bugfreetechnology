What is Physiotherappy?

Physiotherapy often abbreviated as PT is the attempted remediation to individuals and population to develop, maintain, and restore maximum movement and functional ability throughout the life span.

The physiotherapy center strives with identifying and maximizing quantity of life and movement potential within the spheres of promotion, prevention, intervention/ treatment, habilitation and rehabilitation. It insists on healthy interaction with the patients, care givers, family members, and other health professionals and staff where the assessment is done and goals are fixed depending upon the knowledge and skills of the physiotherapist.

Its area of expertise include stroke rehabilitation, rehabilitation of neuromuscular and musculoskeletal disorders and sports related injury, computer and posture related problems, rehabilitation programs for patients undergoing deformity correction, antenatal and postnatal programs, post operative programs for patients undergoing total hip replacements, total knee replacements, patellectomy, as well as post operative cardiac rehabilitation.

What is Lumbago?

Lumbago is a musculoskeletal disorder also known as lower back pain. It is one of the most frequent problems worldwide and is also the second most common cause of a medical visit. The lower back sustains most of the body weight and is involved in almost every movement. A pain in a lower back can be provoked by a muscle or else a ligament sprain and if together then it will be called as a back strain. The Lumbago pain can be continuous and eases by changing body positions or else by performing some physical activity.

Lumbago is of three types depending on how long symptoms hang around:

  • Acute lumbago - it is a pain which can last for about six weeks and can range from mild discomfort to inability to perform any everyday activity.
  • Subacute lumbago - this pain can last for six weeks to three months. It is very important to treat this condition as if ignored it can become chronic.
  • Chronic lumbago - it lasts for more than three months and treatment of this condition is required a multidisciplinary approach.

Causes of Lumbago

Many times causes of Lumbago are very difficult to pinpoint, even after going through all the medical tests. It can be caused by many factors; however, the prime cause is overuse of the lower back or else sudden lifting of heavy objects. It can also be the result of the excessive bending of lower back or else many other repetitive motions which involves the lower back. Many other causes of lower back pain, i.e., Lumbago is

  • Osteoporosis
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Scoliosis
